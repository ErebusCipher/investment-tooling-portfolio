# Kill Step — Can the Hyperliquid Thesis Be Invalidated?
*Pipeline Step 8 | Adversarial Bear | 2026-03-17*

---

## Objective

This step attempts to kill the thesis entirely — not to find improvements, but to identify scenarios where the investment is structurally wrong from the ground up.

---

## Kill Argument 1: The Revenue Is Not Real in the Way the Memo Implies

The memo treats $659M annualised revenue as comparable to Coinbase revenue. It is not. Coinbase revenue is diverse (staking, custody, institutional services, subscriptions) and largely uncorrelated within itself. Hyperliquid's revenue is 100% derived from perpetuals trading fees — a product that is:

(a) Correlated with market volatility and leverage sentiment, which are cyclical and compress severely in bear markets
(b) Subject to competitive undercutting at any time — taker fees are a pricing lever, not a structural moat
(c) Entirely dependent on trader activity on one platform

The 2022 bear market saw on-chain DEX volumes fall 80–95%. If Hyperliquid's $62M/month revenue compresses to $6–12M/month in a sustained bear market, the annualised revenue base drops to $72–144M. At MCap P/S, that's 67–134x. At FDV P/S, that's 270–540x. The memo assumes current revenue is representative without providing a bear market revenue scenario.

**Verdict on kill probability:** Partial. The protocol has shown resilience across market conditions since 2024, but a prolonged bear market (BTC <$40K sustained) would materially destroy the valuation case.

---

## Kill Argument 2: The "No VC Overhang" Narrative Is Obscuring Real Sell Pressure

The memo repeatedly frames "no VC overhang" as the primary structural positive. But the token table shows:
- Team: ~23.8% of 1B = ~238M HYPE, 4-year vest → ~60M tokens/year entering market
- Community/airdrop: already distributed, but recipients may sell
- Ecosystem/emissions: unquantified in this memo

At $40.72/token, 60M tokens/year from team vesting alone = ~$2.4B/year in potential selling pressure. The Assistance Fund buyback at ~$659M revenue (assuming 100% to AF, which is unconfirmed) buys back ~$659M in HYPE. Net: team vesting creates ~$1.75B/year more sell pressure than the buyback covers at current prices.

This is an approximation (actual team vest rate, AF allocation %, and emission schedule require the full tokenomics document), but the directional concern is real. The memo acknowledges "76% overhang" but frames it as a slow-moving risk — the math above suggests it could be more acute than presented.

**Verdict on kill probability:** Medium. Requires full tokenomics breakdown to resolve. Cannot confirm or kill with available data.

---

## Kill Argument 3: Regulatory Risk Is Existential, Not Manageable

The memo categorises regulatory risk as "high" but "not imminent." This framing is drawn from the current political environment (crypto-friendly White House, 2025–2026). It is contingent on politics, not law. The legal framework has not changed:

- On-chain perpetuals are derivatives under the Commodity Exchange Act
- Offering unregistered derivatives to US persons is illegal regardless of administration
- Front-end geo-blocks are trivially circumvented; enforcement actions typically target the protocol itself or founders

The key kill scenario: a CFTC enforcement action against Hyperliquid's founders (who are identifiable US-adjacent persons) in a future administration. This is not a tail risk — it is the base case for any compliant regulatory interpretation. The "crypto-friendly environment" is a cyclical political condition, not a legal one.

If enforcement happens: front-end blocked for Western users, liquidity migrates, volume collapses. The protocol survives but revenue drops 50–70% as the US + EU trader base fragments.

**Verdict on kill probability:** High on a 3–5 year horizon. Not imminent on a 12–18 month view. The memo's "not imminent" framing is fair for near-term, but this risk is not properly discounted for a long-duration position.

---

## Kill Argument 4: HyperEVM Has Not Generated Any Revenue Yet

The memo cites HyperEVM as a "second growth curve" and "primary ecosystem expansion catalyst." HyperEVM launched in 2025 and has not yet produced any measurable protocol revenue beyond the perps/spot trading fees that were already being generated. The memo conflates a launched product with a proven revenue stream. At time of writing, HyperEVM is infrastructure with potential — not a demonstrated revenue driver.

Attributing multiple expansion to HyperEVM without any data on TVL deployed to HyperEVM apps, fees generated by HyperEVM applications, or developer activity is speculative forward guidance, not analysis.

**Verdict on kill probability:** Not a kill on its own — HyperEVM could become material. But it should not be included as a confirmed catalyst; it is a watch item.

---

## Kill Argument 5: Market Share Is Exposed to One Concentrated Risk

>50% on-chain perps market share is a moat, but it is also a concentration risk. If Binance launches an on-chain perps product on BNB Chain with native CEX liquidity depth and cross-margin with spot holdings, the migration incentive for active traders is enormous. Hyperliquid's moat is infrastructure lead — but BNB Chain can add validators and throughput faster than Hyperliquid can add Binance's user base. This has not happened. But the memo rates it "low" without explaining why — it deserves at least a "medium" with a discussion of switching costs.

**Verdict on kill probability:** Low near-term. Medium 2–3 year. The memo's rating is directionally right but underargued.

---

## Overall Kill Assessment

The thesis is **not killed** — but it is narrowed:

- The investment is a 12–18 month trade, not a 4-year hold
- Revenue quality is highly cyclical; the $659M annualised figure needs a bear scenario
- Regulatory risk is existential on a 3–5 year view
- Team vesting sell pressure may be underestimated relative to the AF buyback
- HyperEVM is a watch item, not a confirmed catalyst

**Adjusted stance: Buy with a defined 12–18 month hold horizon. Size reflects regulatory tail risk. Do not treat as a long-duration core position without quarterly revenue monitoring and full tokenomics verification.**
