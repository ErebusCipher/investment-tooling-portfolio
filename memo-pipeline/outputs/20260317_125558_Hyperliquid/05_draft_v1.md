## 1. Business Overview

Hyperliquid is a purpose-built Layer 1 blockchain designed to operate on-chain financial markets at centralised exchange performance levels. The core infrastructure:

- **HyperBFT consensus:** Proprietary BFT variant achieving <1 second block finality at 100,000 orders/second throughput
- **Fully on-chain order book:** Every order, cancel, trade, and liquidation is transparent and verifiable on-chain
- **HyperEVM:** An EVM-compatible execution layer launched in 2025, enabling third-party financial application deployment on the same chain where the liquidity lives
- **HLP (Hyperliquidity Provider):** Protocol-owned liquidity vault that acts as market maker/counterparty, capturing a portion of taker spread

**Token launch (November 2024):** No VC allocation, no institutional pre-sale. ~31% distributed to community via airdrop based on historical protocol usage. Team allocation: ~23.8%, 4-year vest. This is the cleanest token structure in DeFi.

**Supply overview:**
- Circulating: 238.4M (23.8% of max)
- Total: 962.3M
- Max: 1.0B

**Assistance Fund (AF):** A portion of protocol revenue is deployed to buy HYPE off open markets. At $659M annualised revenue, the buyback creates a sustained structural bid. The question is whether buyback pace exceeds emission pace over the vesting schedule — this is the key variable to monitor quarterly.

**Emission schedule risk:** The gap between circulating (238.4M) and total (962.3M) is 724M HYPE. At current price ($40.72), this represents ~$29.5B in potential future supply. Revenue-funded buybacks and price appreciation are the two mechanisms that absorb this — both require the thesis to remain intact.

## 5. Competitive Position

**On-chain perps market share:** >50% of global on-chain perpetuals volume. Nearest competitors are all sub-10%.

**Moat sources:**
1. *Liquidity flywheel:* Deep order book attracts traders → more volume → deeper book. Switching is costly.
2. *Infrastructure advantage:* HyperBFT throughput and latency are not easily replicated. No existing L1 or L2 comes close on perps-specific performance.
3. *No VC overhang:* Token structure means no institutional cliff events to manage against. Community holds majority float.

**Competitive threats:**
- CEX-backed on-chain exchange (Binance/OKX launching native L1 perps). Has not happened; no confirmed roadmap.
- Alternative on-chain perps on Solana (Drift, Zeta): gaining traction in Solana ecosystem but different user base.
- dYdX v4 (Cosmos-based): declining market share, not a near-term threat.

## 7. Risk Assessment

**High:**
- FDV dilution — 76% of supply not yet circulating; 4+ year absorption period
- Regulatory risk — on-chain perps remain unregistered CFTC derivatives; enforcement action could impair access

**Medium:**
- Centralisation — small, permissioned validator set; team retains significant protocol governance
- Protocol risk — HyperBFT is novel; HyperEVM is newly launched; undiscovered vulnerabilities
- Revenue concentration — >90% of revenue from perps; correlated with leverage market conditions

**Low:**
- CEX competitor (not confirmed, infrastructure takes 2+ years to build)
- Smart money exit (Galaxy Digital accumulating, not distributing)

## 8. Investment Conclusion

Hyperliquid is the clearest case in crypto for a protocol that has achieved product-market fit with measurable, growing revenue and a defensible competitive position. The token structure is the best in DeFi. The revenue is real.

The investment requires accepting two material risks: supply dilution at scale (manageable if revenue continues to grow) and regulatory exposure to on-chain derivatives (not manageable by the protocol, but also not imminent). Neither of these risks is binary or immediate.

**Stance: Buy.**
- Entry: current market (~$40–41)
- Near-term target: $41.16 (90d high)
- Medium-term target: $59.30 (ATH)
- Thesis invalidation: monthly revenue sustained below $40M, or regulatory enforcement event impacting front-end access
