# Research Synthesis — Hyperliquid (HYPE)
*Pipeline Step 3 | 2026-03-17*

---

## Protocol Overview

Hyperliquid is a purpose-built Layer 1 blockchain optimised for on-chain derivatives trading. It operates a proprietary consensus mechanism (HyperBFT), processes up to 100,000 orders per second, and achieves sub-1-second block finality — performance benchmarks previously exclusive to centralised exchanges. The primary product is a fully on-chain perpetuals DEX, with spot trading and HyperEVM (an EVM-compatible execution environment) as secondary surfaces.

**Structural differentiator:** No venture capital funding, no institutional pre-sale. ~31% of HYPE supply was distributed via community airdrop at launch (November 2024). Team allocation (~23.8%) is on a 4-year vest. This structure eliminates the VC overhang dynamic that has been the primary kill mechanism for mid-cap DeFi tokens this cycle.

---

## Market Position

Hyperliquid commands >50% of global on-chain perpetuals volume. Nearest competitors — dYdX, GMX, Vertex, Drift — are all sub-10% by volume share. The product won the on-chain perps market by solving the two problems that previously kept traders on CEXs: latency and UX. Block latency <1 second with a CEX-grade order book interface.

On-chain perps still represent a small fraction of total perps market ($3–5T/day on Binance alone). The long-duration thesis is on-chain venues capturing a growing share as infrastructure matures. Hyperliquid is the infrastructure that would need to exist for that shift to happen.

**MCap P/S of 14.7x** is roughly in line with Coinbase (15–20x) — a regulated, fully-diluted, publicly-traded exchange. HYPE is generating comparable revenue to a mid-tier centralized exchange with a fraction of the market infrastructure overhead.

**FDV P/S of 59.3x** is the number that demands a bear case. Only 23.8% of max supply is circulating. The path to full dilution plays out over 4+ years, but this is the correct denominator for long-duration capital allocation.

## Token Mechanics

The **Assistance Fund (AF)** buys HYPE off-market using a portion of trading fees, creating a structural bid. At $659M annualised revenue, the buyback rate is meaningful relative to circulating market cap, but whether it outpaces the emission schedule over time is a key swing variable that requires ongoing monitoring.

**Supply dilution path:** 238.4M circulating → 1.0B max. 76.2% of supply not yet in market. Early-cycle price appreciation compresses FDV P/S ratios faster if revenue grows; the inverse is also true.
