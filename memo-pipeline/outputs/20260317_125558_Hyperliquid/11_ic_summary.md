The edge is not informational superiority about the business. The edge is **time-bounded discipline**: we own the trade the market is pricing and exit before the FDV reality asserts itself.

### Bull / Bear (IC Debate Version)

**Bull — what you say when pushed:**
Real revenue ($659M annualised) at dominant market share (>50% on-chain perps) with no VC distribution pressure. The MCap multiple is defensible against public exchange comps on a 12–18 month view. We are not claiming this is cheap on FDV — we are claiming the market will continue to anchor on circulating supply for the duration of our hold, and that the revenue quality justifies that multiple.

**Bear — what they'll come at you with:**

*"Why doesn't vesting kill this?"*
At current revenue, it doesn't fully. But it suppresses. The trade is bounded by when the vesting pressure — net of buybacks — starts to visibly cap appreciation. That's a 2027+ dynamic, not a 2026 one. We exit before it asserts.

*"Why are you confident on regulatory timing?"*
We're not. We're saying there is no current evidence of near-term enforcement action, not that enforcement is impossible. If that changes — front-end geo-block, CFTC filing — we exit. The position size reflects this tail.

*"What do you know that the market doesn't?"*
Structurally: nothing. The edge is process — explicit time horizon, defined invalidation, position sized to regulatory tail. Most buyers of HYPE are not running a kill step.
