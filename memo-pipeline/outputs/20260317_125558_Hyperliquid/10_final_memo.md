## 1. Protocol Overview

Hyperliquid is a Layer 1 blockchain built specifically for on-chain financial markets, achieving performance benchmarks previously exclusive to centralised exchanges — sub-1-second block finality at 100,000 orders/second throughput — via a proprietary consensus mechanism (HyperBFT). Every order, cancellation, trade, and liquidation is transparent and verifiable on-chain in real time.

**Products:**
- Perpetuals DEX (primary) — fully on-chain order book, CEX-grade UX
- Spot DEX
- HyperEVM — EVM-compatible execution layer for third-party financial applications, natively co-located with protocol liquidity (watch item; no fee revenue confirmed yet)

**Token launch (November 2024):** No VC, no institutional pre-sale. ~31% airdropped to community. Team: ~23.8% of supply on 4-year vest. The Assistance Fund (AF) deploys a portion of protocol revenue to buy HYPE on open markets. The full revenue allocation split (AF vs. operations vs. ecosystem) is not publicly disaggregated — this should be verified before sizing a large position.

**However:** Over a 12–18 month trading horizon, markets may continue to anchor on circulating supply multiples. The MCap P/S provides a useful short-term reference — the investment thesis does not require the FDV multiple to compress, only that the market does not re-anchor to it during the hold period.

1. Revenue growing ~50%+ annually (3-year forward P/S ~20x)

## 4. Token Supply & Buyback Dynamics

- Circulating: 238.4M | Total: 962.3M | Max: 1.0B
- Outstanding: ~724M HYPE (~$29.5B at current price)
- Team vest: ~238M tokens over 4 years (~60M/year **potential** flow — actual realised selling depends on vest schedule structure and market conditions)

**The system is structurally net inflationary at current revenue levels.** Team vesting creates up to ~$2.4B/year in potential supply at current price. The AF buyback absorbs ~$659M/year (assuming full revenue deployment, which is unconfirmed). The net is negative: vesting pressure structurally exceeds buyback capacity by ~$1.75B/year at current price and revenue.

This does not kill the 12–18 month trade — vesting overhang is a slow-moving headwind, not a cliff event. But it definitively kills the "no VC overhang" narrative as a long-duration thesis. The trade is bounded precisely because this dynamic will eventually assert itself. Revenue growth is the only mechanism that closes the gap organically: higher revenue → larger AF → more buyback; higher price → lower token cost per buyback dollar.

**Monitoring requirements:**
- AF wallet on-chain (quarterly): is the buyback actually occurring at the implied rate?
- Team vest wallets: any sustained on-market distribution is an exit signal

## 5. Competitive Position

**Market share:** >50% global on-chain perps volume. All competitors sub-10%.

**Moat sources:** Liquidity flywheel (self-reinforcing depth), infrastructure lead (HyperBFT requires 2+ years to replicate), no cliff events in token structure.

**Competitive threats:**
- CEX-backed on-chain product: **Medium, 2–3 year horizon.** No current evidence of near-term competitive displacement. If a major CEX launches a competing L1 perps product, volume compression would likely provide 6–18 months of signal before material impact.
- Solana perps (Drift, Zeta): different user base, not currently taking Hyperliquid share.
- dYdX v4: declining share; not a near-term threat.

On-chain perps remain <1% of global centralised perps volume. The structural shift from CEX to on-chain is directionally positive but early-stage. Multi-year market share trend data was not available in this analysis — this would strengthen the TAM expansion argument and should be included in a full conviction version.
