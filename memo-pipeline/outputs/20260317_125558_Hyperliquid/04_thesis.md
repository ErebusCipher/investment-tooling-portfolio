# Investment Thesis — Hyperliquid (HYPE)
*Pipeline Step 4 | 2026-03-17*

---

## Core Thesis

HYPE is the dominant on-chain financial infrastructure asset with real, growing revenue ($659M annualised), the cleanest token structure in DeFi (no VC, community airdrop, buyback mechanism), and a second-order growth catalyst in HyperEVM that the market is not yet pricing.

At MCap P/S of 14.7x, the protocol is priced roughly in line with Coinbase — a defensible comp for the leading on-chain exchange. The bear case is not valuation at current prices; it is FDV dilution (59.3x P/S on full float) and the structural regulatory risk inherent to on-chain perpetuals as an unregistered derivatives product.

---

## Bull Case

1. **Revenue quality is exceptional.** $659M annualised, 88.6% take rate, growing TVL. No other DeFi protocol is generating this magnitude of protocol revenue.
2. **No VC overhang.** The primary reason DeFi tokens underperform is coordinated institutional exit. HYPE has none. The only sell pressure is organic (team vesting, ecosystem emissions) and it is offset by a structural buyback.
3. **HyperEVM creates a second growth curve.** As a developer platform for financial applications, Hyperliquid can accrue value from a broader DeFi ecosystem — not just perps fees. This is early but real.
4. **Smart money is accumulating.** Galaxy Digital buying $144M over 40 days is a signal that institutional conviction is forming at current prices.
5. **Macro tailwinds.** ETH options flow (Dec 2200/3200 call spread at scale) and major macro fund flipping long Treasuries both point toward a risk-on Q4. On-chain perps volume is highly correlated with alt market momentum.

---

## Bear Case

1. **FDV dilution is the central risk.** 76% of supply not yet circulating. Even with strong buybacks, the emission overhang will suppress price performance relative to revenue growth if the float expands faster than demand.
2. **Regulatory.** CFTC has explicitly targeted on-chain perpetuals. A front-end ban or enforcement action doesn't kill the protocol, but it fragments users and destroys Western institutional adoption narratives.
3. **The comp is wrong.** Comparing to Coinbase at MCap P/S ignores that Coinbase is fully diluted, regulated, and has a defensible moat via custody and compliance infrastructure. HYPE has none of those. The correct discount is significant.

---

## Conviction Level

**Buy** on current prices with awareness of dilution risk.
- Near-term target: $41.16 (90d high / current resistance)
- Medium-term target: $59.30 (ATH — requires revenue trend maintenance and HyperEVM traction)
- Stop thesis: sustained revenue decline below $40M/month, or regulatory enforcement that impairs front-end access
