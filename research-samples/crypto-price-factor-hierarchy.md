# Crypto Price Factor Hierarchy

## How to Use This Document

Every price move in crypto has a cause that sits somewhere in this hierarchy. When something moves unexpectedly, start at Tier 1 and work down until you find it. When building a new position, check which tier the thesis lives in — a Tier 3 thesis in a Tier 1 environment loses.

**The hierarchy matters more than the list.** Tier 1 factors override everything below them. A perfect token-specific thesis gets destroyed by a regulatory enforcement event or a macro liquidity shock. Size and timing decisions should reflect which tier is currently dominant.

---

## Tier 1 — Global Macro & Liquidity
*These factors dominate during stress events. When Tier 1 moves, everything correlates to 1.*

### 1.1 Central Bank Policy
- Fed funds rate direction and forward guidance
- QE / QT pace — net liquidity injection or withdrawal
- Fed balance sheet size and trajectory
- FOMC meeting outcomes vs. market pricing
- Fed pivot signals (tone shifts before actual moves)
- ECB and BOJ policy — yen carry trade dynamics; carry unwind = crypto selloff
- PBOC stimulus cycles — Chinese capital flows into crypto

### 1.2 Global Liquidity
- Global M2 money supply (US + China + EU + Japan combined) — strongest long-run correlation with BTC price
- Cross-border capital flows
- Dollar milkshake dynamics — DXY strength drains EM and risk assets simultaneously
- Eurodollar system tightening / loosening
- Repo market stress — overnight rate spikes = liquidity crunch

### 1.3 Risk Appetite Indicators
- DXY direction (inverse to crypto in risk-off; breaks down in inflation regimes)
- US 10y real yield (TIPS) — rising real rates = opportunity cost rising
- Credit spreads (HY OAS) — widening = risk-off
- VIX level and direction
- Nasdaq / S&P 500 correlation — crypto runs ~0.6–0.8 beta to Nasdaq in normal regimes, spikes to 1.0 in stress
- Private credit market conditions — redemption waves signal broader liquidity stress
- Carry trade positioning (JPY/USD) — unwinds hit crypto hard and fast

### 1.4 Macro Data
- CPI / PCE (inflation above target = Fed hawkish = risk-off)
- NFP / unemployment (strong = risk-on; very strong = hawkish Fed = risk-off)
- GDP growth vs. recession probability
- China economic data (property sector, PMI) — proxy for global growth demand

## Tier 2 — Political & Regulatory
*Binary events. Can move prices 20–50% overnight. Cannot be hedged with size alone — requires position limits on affected assets.*

### 2.1 US Political Environment
- Presidential administration stance on crypto (pro vs. hostile)
- Strategic Bitcoin Reserve — policy and execution
- Congressional legislation: market structure clarity, stablecoin bill
- Treasury / FinCEN stance on crypto businesses
- Presidential election cycles — major regime changes in regulatory posture
- Executive orders affecting crypto

### 2.2 Regulatory Enforcement
- SEC enforcement actions (exchange shutdowns, token classification as securities)
- CFTC actions (perps, derivatives)
- DOJ / FBI seizures and prosecutions
- Exchange regulatory status (Coinbase, Binance, Kraken licensing events)
- Stablecoin regulation (USDT/USDC status affects entire market liquidity)
- OFAC sanctions on wallet addresses
- Bank access for crypto businesses (Fed master account denial = liquidity constraint)
- Basel III crypto capital requirements for banks

### 2.3 Global Regulatory Divergence
- EU MiCA implementation and compliance pressure
- Hong Kong / Singapore licensing regimes (institutional gateway)
- UAE / Bahrain regulatory arbitrage
- China ban/unban cycles — historically drives 30–40% moves
- India, South Korea, Brazil regulatory shifts
- Global regulatory coordination risk (G20 / FATF standards)
- Nation-state adoption events (El Salvador-type — directionally positive)

### 2.4 Geopolitical
- Major conflicts affecting risk appetite (global safe haven flows)
- Sanctions regimes driving BTC demand in sanctioned countries
- US-China tension (decoupling narrative = BTC as neutral reserve asset)
- EM currency crises (dollarization → BTC demand, but also capital controls limiting access)
- Energy price shocks (mining cost floor effect on BTC)

## Tier 3 — Crypto Market Structure & Capital Flows
*Determines the amplitude of moves. Tier 1 sets direction; Tier 3 determines how far.*

### 3.1 Institutional Capital
- Spot BTC ETF flows (daily net inflows/outflows)
- Spot ETH ETF flows
- Grayscale trust discount/premium cycles
- Hedge fund crypto allocation mandates
- Endowment / pension allocation decisions
- Corporate treasury adoption
- Prime brokerage availability and terms

### 3.2 Retail Participation
- Coinbase app store ranking (proxy for retail re-entry)
- Google Trends: "buy bitcoin", "crypto" search volume
- Exchange new account registrations
- Retail stablecoin on-ramp volumes
- Fear & Greed Index (extreme readings = contrarian signal)

### 3.3 Leverage and Derivatives
- Aggregate open interest — rising OI + rising price = leveraged long building
- Perpetual funding rates — sustained positive = crowded long; negative = capitulation or squeeze setup
- Options positioning: gamma exposure, max pain, delta hedging flows
- Options skew (put/call ratio, vol skew) — institutional hedging demand
- Liquidation levels and cascade risk
- Basis (futures premium/discount to spot) — institutional demand indicator

*See [[17 - Derivatives Positioning Signals]] for full treatment.*

### 3.4 Stablecoin Dynamics
- Total stablecoin market cap trend — growing = dry powder; shrinking = capital leaving
- Exchange stablecoin inflows — leading indicator of buy pressure
- USDT/USDC minting/burning rates
- Stablecoin yield relative to TradFi — affects capital allocation decisions

### 3.5 Exchange and Custody Flows
- Exchange BTC/ETH reserves (falling = accumulation; rising = distribution)
- Whale wallet net flows (on-chain large address movements)
- Miner outflows (cost-of-production selling pressure)
- OTC desk activity (block trades, dark pool equivalent)

### 3.6 VC and Private Market Pipeline
- Private funding round pace and valuations (leading indicator of token supply 12–24 months out)
- VC lock-up expiry calendars
- TGE pipeline — upcoming launches competing for capital
- Foundation / ecosystem grant distributions
- Protocol treasury selling activity

*See [[16 - Pre-TGE Supply Intelligence]] for full treatment.*

## Tier 4 — Crypto Narrative Cycles
*The dominant layer for mid-cap and small-cap price action. Narrative determines which sector gets allocated to; fundamentals determine which token within that sector.*

### 4.1 Macro Crypto Narratives (cycle-level)
- BTC as digital gold / inflation hedge / monetary reserve asset
- BTC as institutional treasury reserve
- ETH as programmable settlement layer / ultrasound money
- Crypto as dollar alternative in EM (remittance, dollarization)
- Web3 / decentralisation thesis (secular)

### 4.2 Sector Narratives (rotate within cycles)
| Narrative | Typical Phase | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| AI × crypto (agents, inference, private compute) | Mid-cycle | Requires macro tailwind |
| RWA tokenisation | Mid-cycle | Institutional-driven |
| DePIN (decentralised physical infrastructure) | Early-mid | Capital-intensive |
| Modular / DA layer | Late-cycle tell | Peaked 2023–24 cycle |
| Alt L1 | Late-cycle tell | Peaked 2021 cycle |
| L2 scaling | Late-cycle tell | Peaked 2023 cycle |
| DeFi | Ranging/bear | Rebasing not re-rating |
| NFT / gaming / metaverse | Very late cycle | Distribution signal |
| Meme coins | Peak = distribution warning | Confirms cycle top |
| Privacy / confidential compute | Emerging | Nascent |

### 4.3 Narrative Rotation Mechanics
- Capital concentration — where is Crypto Twitter / Kaito narrative score pointing?
- Conference calendar — ETHDenver, Consensus, Token2049 concentrate attention
- KOL positioning — not alpha, but moves retail flows
- Research publication cycles (Messari, Delphi, Bankless) — narrative amplifiers
- Mainstream media coverage = late-cycle signal

*See [[05 - Narrative Capture and Sector Rotation]] for full treatment.*

### 4.4 BTC Halving Cycle
- Pre-halving accumulation phase
- Post-halving supply shock (mining reward reduction)
- Historical pattern: BTC ATH ~12–18 months post-halving
- Alt season typically follows BTC ATH by 3–6 months
- Use as a probabilistic envelope, not a deterministic calendar

## Tier 5 — Token-Specific Fundamentals
*Most important for position sizing and conviction within a narrative. A bad token in a hot narrative underperforms; a good token in a dead narrative also underperforms. Both matter.*

### 5.1 Revenue and Cash Flows
- Protocol fee revenue (30d, 90d trend) — only reliable fundamental anchor in crypto
- Take rate (what % of fees accrue to protocol vs. LPs)
- Revenue source diversification (single-product risk vs. multi-product)
- Revenue cyclicality (perps revenue collapses in bear; lending revenue is counter-cyclical)
- Fee switch status (does revenue flow to token holders or just treasury?)
- Buyback mechanics (AF-style buybacks = indirect value accrual)
- P/S multiple vs. comparable protocols

### 5.2 Usage and Adoption
- TVL trend (absolute and vs. ATH)
- Daily/monthly active addresses or users
- Transaction volume
- Developer activity (GitHub commits, unique contributors)
- Protocol integrations
- API call volume / off-chain usage metrics

### 5.3 Supply Dynamics
- Circulating vs. max supply (float percentage)
- Vesting schedules — team, investors, foundation
- Unlock cliff dates and magnitude
- Emission rate / inflation (annual % increase in supply)
- Net supply change (inflation minus burns minus buybacks)
- VC allocation and lock-up status
- Foundation/treasury selling behaviour
- Hidden inflation risk (parachain emissions, ecosystem grants not disclosed)

*See [[16 - Pre-TGE Supply Intelligence]] for pre-launch supply analysis.*

### 5.4 Competitive Moat
- Market share within sector (is this the dominant protocol?)
- Liquidity flywheel (does scale create defensible advantage)
- Switching costs (how hard for users to migrate)
- Network effects (does each new user make it more valuable)
- Protocol composability (DeFi legos — being the base layer others build on)
- First-mover advantage vs. fast-follower risk

### 5.5 Team and Execution
- Founder track record
- Technical credibility
- Delivery record
- Communication quality (transparent, data-driven vs. hype-driven)
- Team retention
- Investor backing quality (a16z, Paradigm signal — but also signals VC supply pressure)

### 5.6 Catalysts and Timing
- Named partnership with recognisable counterparty
- Exchange listing
- Mainnet launch / major upgrade
- Fee switch activation
- ETF inclusion potential
- Protocol merger or acquisition
- Regulatory clarity event
- Token burn or buyback program announcement

*See [[08 - New Coins and Catalysts as Alpha]] for catalyst analysis.*

### 5.7 Risk Factors (token-specific)
- Smart contract audit status
- Bridge security
- Oracle dependency
- Governance attack surface
- Centralisation risk (validator set concentration, multisig control)
- Regulatory classification risk (is this token likely a security?)
- Ecosystem dependency

## Tier 6 — Technical / Momentum
*Lowest tier fundamentally, but highest frequency. Governs entry and exit timing within a thesis. Never a primary reason to own something — but ignoring it costs P&L.*

### 6.1 Price Momentum
- 1m / 3m / 6m price momentum (strongest documented factor in crypto)
- BTC relative strength (is BTC leading or lagging alts)
- Sector rotation (which sector is outperforming BTC this week)
- 52-week high proximity (breakout = momentum continuation signal)
- Mean reversion vs. trend continuation (regime-dependent)

### 6.2 On-Chain Technical
- Realised price (average cost basis of all holders) — support/resistance level
- MVRV ratio (market value vs. realised value — overextension indicator)
- SOPR (spent output profit ratio) — are holders selling at profit or loss
- Accumulation/distribution address cohorts
- Exchange inflow/outflow spikes (sudden large inflows = sell pressure incoming)

### 6.3 Derivatives Technical
- Funding rate extremes (>0.1% per 8h = overheated; negative = fear)
- Open interest vs. price divergence (rising price, falling OI = weak momentum)
- Options gamma walls (price gravitates toward max pain before expiry)
- Basis convergence (futures discount closing = spot demand absorbing)

## Cross-Factor Interactions — Where the Edge Is

The factors don't operate independently. These interactions are where the edge is:

**Macro × Narrative**
A strong narrative in a tightening macro environment underperforms. Timing narrative exposure to coincide with liquidity expansion is where the returns are, not just picking the right narrative. See [[03 - Regime-Dependent Factor Dominance and Reflexivity]].

**Regulatory × Token Fundamentals**
Real revenue doesn't protect against regulatory enforcement. The best revenue in DeFi and a CFTC enforcement event cuts it 50%+ regardless. Regulatory risk discounts all token-specific quality below a certain size.

**Unlock Calendar × Narrative Strength**
A large unlock into a hot narrative gets absorbed. A large unlock into a fading narrative accelerates the selloff. The unlock doesn't cause the move — the narrative environment determines whether it matters. See [[16 - Pre-TGE Supply Intelligence]].

**Leverage × Momentum**
Rising price + rising open interest + positive funding = leveraged long crowding. This setup both amplifies gains and creates liquidation cascade risk. See [[17 - Derivatives Positioning Signals]].

**Institutional Flows × Retail Sentiment**
ETF inflows set the bid underneath the market; retail sentiment determines volatility and overshoot. Institutional accumulation in sideways market → eventual retail FOMO → blow-off top. Knowing where in this sequence you are determines whether you add or trim.

## Factor Hierarchy Summary

| Tier | Category | Typical Impact | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Global macro / liquidity | 30–70% portfolio moves | Weeks–months |
| 2 | Political / regulatory | 20–50% single-asset moves | Event-driven, unpredictable |
| 3 | Market structure / flows | 10–30% amplitude effects | Daily–weekly |
| 4 | Narrative cycles | 50–300% sector moves | Months–quarters |
| 5 | Token fundamentals | Determines relative performance within sector | Quarters |
| 6 | Technical / momentum | 5–15% entry/exit timing | Daily |

**The key insight:** Most retail and even sophisticated investors spend most of their time at Tier 5 and 6. Professional process is weighting Tier 1–3 correctly before making Tier 5 decisions. A Tier 5 thesis that ignores Tier 1 conditions is an incomplete thesis.
